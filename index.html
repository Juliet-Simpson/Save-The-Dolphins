The Ganges Dolphin

is one of only three species of fresh water dolphin left, since the extinction of the Yangtze Dolphin. Once present in tens of thousands, its numbers are dropping at a worrying rate, from about 4500 as recently as 1982 to an estimated 1800 now. There are many strains on the population, including dam building, pollution and depletion of river water.
